Antipsychotics & Antidepressants — Dose Range & Receptor Occupancy

Usual adult oral dose vs primary-target occupancy — striatal D2 (antipsychotics) · SERT/NET/DAT (antidepressants). A quick-reference aid; verify locally before prescribing.

Antipsychotics — the 65–80% D2 window

Illustrative pattern — not measured data. Thresholds (65 / 72 / 80% D2) from Kapur 2000 & Seeman 2002.

Antidepressants — ~80% SERT at the minimum dose

Illustrative pattern — not measured data. ~80% SERT at the minimum therapeutic dose from Meyer 2004 & Sørensen 2021 (SNRIs add NET higher up; bupropion ~26% DAT).

How to read the occupancy column

65% D2 → antipsychotic response 72% D2 → prolactin ↑ 80% D2 → EPS 80% SERT → antidepressant efficacy
  • partial agonist can exceed 90% D2 without EPS (intrinsic activity) — occupancy alone doesn't predict EPS here.
  • fast-off low/transient D2 despite antipsychotic effect (clozapine, quetiapine) — little EPS/prolactin.
  • low / non-D2 mesolimbic-preferential (lumateperone) or no D2 at all (pimavanserin).

CRITICAL SAFETY — DOSES & COMBINATIONS TO AVOID

🚫 MAOI + serotonergic → serotonin syndrome

Never combine MAOIs with SSRIs, SNRIs, TCAs (esp. clomipramine), tramadol, linezolid, triptans, St John's wort. Washout ≥14 d — 5 weeks after fluoxetine. MAOI + tyramine → hypertensive crisis.

🚫 Don't chase D2 > 80%

Above the 65–80% window you add EPS, akathisia and prolactin — not efficacy (Kapur 2000). Partial agonists & clozapine/quetiapine are the exceptions, read by mechanism not mg.

⚠ QT dose caps & monitoring

Citalopram max 40 mg (20 mg elderly/CYP2C19-poor/hepatic). Watch ziprasidone, iloperidone, thioridazine, pimozide, IV haloperidol; don't stack QT-prolongers — check ECG, K⁺, Mg²⁺.

⚠ Clozapine hazards

Agranulocytosis, myocarditis, seizures, constipation/ileus. Mandatory ANC monitoring (REMS); reserved for treatment resistance.

⚠ TCA overdose lethality

Cardiac Na-channel blockade → lethal in overdose. Dispense cautiously in at-risk patients; avoid in conduction disease / recent MI.

⚠ Serotonin syndrome — not only MAOIs

Any serotonergic combination can trigger it (e.g. SSRI/SNRI + tramadol, triptan, or linezolid). Review the whole regimen, not just the antidepressant.
