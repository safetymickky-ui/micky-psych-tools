Receptor-Binding Fingerprints — Antipsychotics & Antidepressants

In-vitro affinity across the full receptor panel — the fingerprint behind the side-effect profile. Companion to the dose–occupancy reference. Read affinity WITH dose and functional direction.

How to read — three things that are NOT the same

  1. Affinity ≠ occupancy ≠ effect. Low Ki ≠ engagement at therapeutic dose (depends on dose/level/penetration).
  2. Affinity ≠ functional direction. Same bin = antagonism / partial agonism / agonism / inverse agonism. D2 +++ is prolactin-raising for risperidone but prolactin-sparing for aripiprazole. Read the Signature.
  3. Bins are coarse ordinal buckets, not a linear scale.

⛔ CRITICAL SAFETY — WHAT THE FINGERPRINTS IMPLY TO AVOID

🚫 MAOI + serotonergic → serotonin syndrome

MAOI + any serotonergic agent = serotonin syndrome (potentially fatal).

⚠ Anticholinergic (M1) burden stacking

Anticholinergic burden stacking (additive M1 blockade -> confusion/delirium, constipation/paralytic ileus, urinary retention, blurred vision, tachycardia, falls. worst in the elderly and in dementia).

⚠ QT prolongation — hERG, not in this panel

QT prolongation / torsades - a hERG(IKr) channel effect NOT captured by this receptor panel, so it must be tracked separately.

⚠ Orthostatic hypotension — additive α1 block

Orthostatic hypotension and fall/fracture risk, especially in the elderly - additive alpha1 blockade.

⚠ Clozapine-specific hazards

Clozapine-specific hazards requiring active monitoring, not just avoidance: agranulocytosis (mandatory ANC/REMS monitoring), myocarditis (early) and cardiomyopathy, dose-dependent seizures, severe constipation/ileus (anticholinergic + hypomotility - a leading cause of clozapine death), sialorrhea (M4 partial agonism), orthostasis on titration, and metabolic syndrome.

⚠ Hyperprolactinaemia — pure D2 antagonists

Hyperprolactinemia: pure high-affinity D2 antagonists (risperidone, paliperidone, amisulpride, and the high-potency FGAs haloperidol/fluphenazine) raise prolactin most (galactorrhea, amenorrhea, sexual dysfunction, long-term bone loss).

Clinical reference aid — not a substitute for clinical judgment or local protocol. In-vitro affinities (Ki); read with dose, occupancy and functional direction. QT/hERG and cardiac Na-channel effects are NOT in this receptor panel.
