Perioperative Management of Pheochromocytoma & Sympathetic Paraganglioma (PPGL)

Surgical-safety reference for elective resection of functional (catecholamine-secreting) PPGL in adults · basis: Endocrine Society clinical practice guideline + the PRESCRIPT randomized trial · for the anaesthetist / endocrinologist / surgeon team

α-blockade first, always before any β-blocker — titrate over ~7–14 days to seated BP <130/80 mmHg & HR 60–70 bpm, with sodium + fluid loading. In theatre expect catecholamine surges on tumour manipulation, then an abrupt crash after venous ligation; then watch 24–48 h for hypotension & rebound hypoglycaemia. Confidence: moderate-high.

Choosing the α-blocker — start 2–3 weeks pre-op, BP-titrated

If: most reactive tumour · tightest intra-op control
Phenoxybenzamine — non-selective, irreversible
Trade-off: more & more protracted post-op hypotension; longer stay (12.2 vs 6.2 days).
If: cleaner recovery · fewer side effects
Doxazosin — selective α1
Trade-off: more intra-op hypotension (SBP<80 in 67% vs 35%); more post-op pressor / ICU.
PRESCRIPT RCT (n=134): either is acceptable — time outside BP target 11.1% vs 12.2% (P=.75); instability score favoured phenoxybenzamine (38 vs 50); 30-day CV complications similar (8.8% vs 6.9%).

Set-up

Radial arterial line + large-bore/central access. Laparoscopic for most pheochromocytomas, open for most paragangliomas. First move on a surge: tell surgeon to stop manipulating.

▲ Surge agents — short-acting, titratable

  • Phentolamine — 5 mg IV bolus, repeat p.r.n.
  • Nicardipine — 5 → max 15 mg/h
  • Nitroglycerin — 5 → 100 µg/min
  • Magnesium sulfate — 4 g in 250 mL D5W (≤3 mL/min), refractory
  • Esmolol — 0.5–1 mg/kg then 50–150 µg/kg/min (tachyarrhythmia; α-blocked only)

▼ Crash — after venous ligation

Pre-emptive IV isotonic fluid is the mainstay; add norepinephrine or vasopressin 0.03–0.04 units/min for catecholamine-resistant vasoplegia. Pure α-agonists (phenylephrine) are of limited use — α-receptors are still blocked.

3 Post-operative 24–48 h

Watch closely 24–48 h

ICU / high-dependency for higher-risk cases. Post-surgical complication rate ~19%; prolonged hypotension is the commonest (~10%).

💧 Hypotension (BP <90/60 mmHg)

Catecholamine withdrawal + residual α-blockade + expanded vascular bed — worst after phenoxybenzamine. Fluids first, then norepinephrine / vasopressin.

🍬 Rebound hypoglycaemia

Rebound hyperinsulinaemia once the tumour is out — typically the first 4 hours. Check glucose every 6 h for ≥24 h; IV 5% dextrose until oral intake; re-adjust insulin in diabetics.

Adrenal insufficiency

Not expected after unilateral adrenalectomy. Steroid-cover only bilateral / cortical-sparing cases or if clinically suspected.

CRITICAL SAFETY — MEDICATIONS / ACTIONS TO AVOID
🚫

β-blocker before α-blockade

Unopposed α-stimulation → hypertensive crisis. α-blockade always first; β-blocker only after, for tachycardia.

🚫

Morphine & codeine

Avoided per Endocrine Society guideline. Fentanyl is tolerated and safe.

🚫

Dopamine antagonists · steroids · TCAs

Metoclopramide & other dopamine-receptor antagonists, corticosteroids, and tricyclics precipitate crisis.

⚠

Unprepared surgery

Operating on an un-optimised functional PPGL — the core avoidable catastrophe and a crisis trigger.

Clinical reference aid — not a substitute for clinical judgment or local protocol. Verify against the primary sources before acting.
