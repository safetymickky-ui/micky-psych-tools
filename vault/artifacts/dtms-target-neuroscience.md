# The Neuroscience of Deep TMS Targets — Why the H-coil Aims Where It Does
*2026-07-17 · deep research · PubMed backbone (four target pillars) layered onto the verified Deep TMS comprehensive review*

> Scope (intent-locked): the **neuroscience of the currently FDA-cleared Deep TMS targets** — depression (H1), anxious depression (H1), OCD (H7), smoking cessation (H4) — read at two levels together, **circuit rationale and biological mechanism**, and closing on where connectivity-guided targeting is heading. This is the *target* companion to the whole-modality [[Deep Transcranial Magnetic Stimulation (Deep TMS) — Comprehensive Review]]; it does not re-litigate protocol efficacy or the "is deeper better?" decision (that is the coil-choice decision report).

## The reframe: a Deep TMS "target" is a field over a network node, not a deep point

The single most important idea in this whole topic is that "deep" and "target" both mislead if read literally. The H-coil (Hesed coil) does not reach down and depolarise a deep grey-matter nucleus. Its windings are spread tangentially over a large scalp area and oriented so the induced electric field summates in one direction while radial/return components cancel, so the field attenuates more slowly with depth than a focal figure-8 coil's tightly localised dipole (Roth 2007, PMID:17277575). At 120% of hand motor threshold the H1 coil induces a *supra-threshold* field in lateral and medial prefrontal cortex to a depth of roughly **4–5 cm**, against roughly **1.5 cm** directly under a figure-8 coil (Roth 2007, PMID:17277575); physiologically, an H-coil activated the abductor-pollicis-brevis motor representation at a coil–cortex distance of **5.5 cm** versus only **2 cm** for figure-8 (Zangen 2005, PMID:15792886).

That reach is bought at a fixed price. Simulating 50 coil designs on a spherical head model, every coil obeyed a **depth–focality trade-off**: deeper penetration obligatorily comes with a wider, less focal field — figure-8-pattern coils reach a tangential spread of ~5 cm² while circular/deep patterns spread to ~34 cm² at comparable depth (Deng 2013, PMID:22483681). Where the field is actually strong enough to fire neurons is still **superficial** — a consensus biophysics position locates the primary physical target at axonal terminals and bends at the gyral crown and juxtacortical white matter, not in deep nuclei. So the mechanistically honest reading of "deep" is: the H-coil engages a **broader expanse of cortex and its connections** (fMRI confirms the H-coil recruits a larger lateral-PFC volume than a figure-8's focal spot — Pell 2023, PMID:36931728), and the therapeutic effect then propagates **trans-synaptically** into limbic and subcortical nodes. Every cleared "target" below is, at bottom, a *cortical entry point chosen for what it is connected to*.

## The plasticity mechanism: LTP/LTD-like change, NMDA/Ca²⁺-gated

The after-effects of repetitive TMS that outlast the stimulation train are modelled on activity-dependent synaptic **LTP/LTD** — the same phenomena first described in hippocampal slices. The cleanest demonstration is theta-burst: **intermittent** theta-burst (iTBS) facilitates corticospinal excitability (LTP-like) while **continuous** theta-burst (cTBS) suppresses it (LTD-like), produced in tens of seconds (Huang 2005, PMID:15664172). By convention high-frequency (~5–20 Hz) rTMS is facilitatory and 1 Hz inhibitory. Two pharmacology experiments pin the synaptic substrate: the NMDA-receptor antagonist **memantine abolished both** cTBS suppression and iTBS facilitation (Huang 2007, PMID:17368094), and the L-type voltage-gated Ca²⁺-channel blocker **nimodipine flipped cTBS from LTD-like to LTP-like** — postsynaptic Ca²⁺ dynamics act as the metaplastic switch that sets the sign of the change (Wankerl 2010, PMID:20445045). Pattern and calcium state, in other words, matter *more than raw frequency* for the direction of plasticity.

The molecular story below that is looser than the textbook version. BDNF is the canonical hypothesised mediator, but a meta-analysis found rTMS did **not** reliably raise serum BDNF (g = −0.12, non-significant) — the human peripheral evidence is weak (Jiang 2019, PMID:31208790). What is well demonstrated is trans-synaptic **neurotransmitter release**: focal motor-cortex rTMS causes measurable **striatal dopamine release** ([¹¹C]raclopride displacement in putamen) along corticostriatal glutamatergic projections (Strafella 2003, PMID:12937078) — direct proof that stimulating one cortical node modulates a distant one through the circuit.

## Depression (H1): the DLPFC–subgenual anticorrelation

The depression target is **bilateral prefrontal cortex with a left-lateralised dorsolateral (DLPFC) emphasis**; the pivotal protocol delivered 18 Hz "over the prefrontal cortex" (Levkovitz 2015, PMID:25655160). Why the DLPFC? Not because the DLPFC is the sick tissue, but because it is an accessible cortical node **inversely coupled to a limbic hub that is over-active in depression**. The landmark finding: across candidate DLPFC sites, antidepressant efficacy tracks the strength of that site's intrinsic **anticorrelation (negative resting-state functional connectivity) with the subgenual anterior cingulate cortex (sgACC)** — the more anticorrelated the site, the better the response, with optimal coordinates falling in Brodmann area 46 (Fox 2012, PMID:22658708). Stimulating a node that is negatively coupled to the hyperactive sgACC down-regulates that deep target through the network.

This is not a retrospective correlation only: in individual patients the sgACC–stimulation-site connectivity was the **only independent predictor** of response and predicted response to **active but not sham** rTMS (Weigand 2018, PMID:29274805), and the anticorrelation is a reproducible, task-robust phenomenon (Li 2020, PMID:33359345). The systems framing is a rebalancing of an over-engaged default-mode network (sgACC/medial PFC) against an under-engaged executive/dorsolateral system. The pivotal RCT (n=212, medication-resistant MDD) showed HDRS-21 improvement of **6.39 vs 3.28 points** (p=0.008; effect size 0.76), **response 38.4% vs 21.4%** (p=0.013) and **remission 32.6% vs 14.6%** (p=0.005), the basis of the **2013 FDA clearance** (Levkovitz 2015, PMID:25655160).

## Anxious depression (H1): the same field, a mirror-image predictor

The **2021 anxious-depression clearance is a label expansion on the same H1 target**, not a new circuit. A pooled analysis of the H1 MDD RCTs found active-vs-sham anxiety improvement of effect size **0.34 (p=0.03) at 4 weeks**, sustained (0.35) to 16 weeks, pooled effect size **0.55**, with H1 outperforming figure-8 (p=0.042) (Pell 2022, PMID:35207288). The striking neuroscience point is a **reversal of the usual predictor direction**: higher baseline anxiety predicted a *better* dTMS outcome — the opposite of the negative-prognostic weight anxious features carry for medication and superficial TMS (Pell 2022, PMID:35207288). Read it as "the prefrontal target also moves the comorbid-anxiety dimension," not as a separate anatomical target.

## OCD (H7): mPFC/dACC as the door into the CSTC loop

The OCD target changes coil and coordinates: the **H7 coil** stimulates **bilateral medial prefrontal cortex (mPFC) and dorsal anterior cingulate cortex (dACC)**, placed ~4 cm anterior to the leg motor cortex, at **20 Hz and 100% of leg resting motor threshold** (Roth 2022, PMID:36040972; Carmi 2019, PMID:31109199). The rationale is the **cortico-striato-thalamo-cortical (CSTC) loop** model: OCD shows hyperactivity of orbitofrontal cortex (OFC), ACC and caudate that normalises with successful SRI/CBT treatment (Hazari 2019, PMID:30745673). mPFC/dACC is chosen as the *superficial, TMS-reachable* node of that otherwise deep loop, and E-field modelling confirms the H7 delivers a supra-threshold field to a meaningful fraction of the loop's cortical extent (~15% of dACC, ~30% of OFC, ~50% of dlPFC, ~78% of pre-SMA volume) — far more than the figure-8 coil later cleared by substantial equivalence (Roth 2022, PMID:36040972).

The dACC anchor has an electrophysiological signature: the **error-related negativity (ERN)**, a theta event-related potential source-localised to dACC, is elevated in OCD patients *and* in their unaffected first-degree relatives (a trait/endophenotype marker), and clinical response to dTMS tracked a **treatment-induced increase in ERN** — evidence the stimulation modified the intended node (Carmi 2018, PMID:28927961). The pivotal 11-site RCT (n=99) showed a Y-BOCS reduction of **6.0 vs 3.3 points**, **response 38.1% vs 11.1%** post-treatment rising to **45.2% vs 17.8%** at one month, the basis of the **2018 FDA De Novo clearance** — the first cleared device for OCD (Carmi 2019, PMID:31109199); benefit held even after multiple medication and CBT failures (Roth 2020, PMID:32540588).

## State-dependent plasticity: why the OCD and smoking protocols provoke first

The OCD and smoking protocols share a design feature absent from the depression protocol: **each session is immediately preceded by individualised provocation** — tailored OCD triggers, or cue-induced craving — to bring the pathological circuit "online" before the pulses arrive. The rationale is **state-dependent plasticity**: an already-activated circuit is more susceptible to modulation than one stimulated at rest (Roth 2022, PMID:36040972). In OCD this has mechanistic support — response correlated with the ERN increase, a direct read-out of the provoked-then-stimulated node (Carmi 2018, PMID:28927961). The caution is that provocation's *clinical* necessity is unproven: a secondary analysis of the smoking trial found the craving induction reliably raised craving (b=0.34) but its **magnitude did not predict quit outcome** (arm interaction p=0.95) (Mehta 2025, PMID:41110551). The principle is sound; the dose–response is not established.

## Addiction / smoking (H4): the insula–prefrontal craving circuit

The **H4 coil** stimulates **bilateral lateral prefrontal cortex *and* bilateral insula** simultaneously at ~10 Hz (Zangen 2021, PMID:34505368) — a two-node target no surface coil can reach, because the insula sits too deep for a figure-8 field. The insula is the crux. Lesion evidence is close to a natural experiment: smokers whose strokes damaged the insula quit "easily, immediately, without relapse, and without persistence of the urge to smoke," implicating the insula as a **critical, possibly necessary** substrate of nicotine addiction (Naqvi 2007, PMID:17255515). Mechanistically the insula recalls **interoceptive representations of the drug effect** and injects them into conscious craving (Naqvi 2014, PMID:24690001), while the lateral/dorsolateral PFC supplies **top-down cognitive control** — 10 Hz to DLPFC alone already reduces consumption (SMD −1.22 in a network meta-analysis; Tseng 2021, PMID:34347916). The H4 co-stimulates the **craving-generating node and the control node** together.

The pivotal double-blind RCT (n=262 chronic smokers; each session after cue provocation) hit its primary endpoint — a 4-week continuous quit rate through week 18 of **19.4% vs 8.7%** in the ITT efficacy set (p=0.017) and **28.0% vs 11.7%** among completers (p=0.007) — the basis of the **August 2020 FDA clearance, the first for any addiction indication** (Zangen 2021, PMID:34505368). (The FDA/BrainsWay full-ITT figures use a slightly different denominator, 17.1% vs 7.9%.) The addiction frontier now runs through the insula: an insula-targeting H-coil variant combined with varenicline reported **82.4% vs 30.7%** abstinence at week 12 in a small RCT — circuit-plus-drug outperforming either alone (Brainsway H-ADD/varenicline RCT, doi:10.1016/j.brs.2023.10.002).

## Where targeting is heading: connectivity-guided, individualized, closed-loop

The Fox/Weigand connectivity logic is the seed of a shift from *anatomical* to *individualized* targeting: pick each patient's DLPFC site by their own resting-state anticorrelation with sgACC rather than a scalp heuristic. The most visible expression is **SAINT/SNT** — fMRI-guided, accelerated (50 sessions/5 days), high-dose stimulation reaching **90.5% open-label remission** (Cole 2020, PMID:32252538) and a sham-controlled MADRS reduction of **52.5% vs 11.1%** (Cole/Williams 2021, PMID:34711062). **Critical distinction: SAINT/SNT uses a focal figure-8 iTBS coil, NOT an H-coil** — it is cited here as the frontier of *targeting philosophy* (individualized + connectivity-guided + accelerated), and it exposes the central tension of the whole field: H-coils deliver **broad, deep, fixed, MRI-free** fields (pragmatic and scalable — the OCD/addiction route), while figure-8 + fcMRI delivers **precise but shallow** targeting (the depression frontier). Convergence — deep *and* individualized *and* **closed-loop** (EEG-informed stimulation timed to high-excitability brain states, which produces greater, more lasting plasticity than fixed open-loop protocols; Zrenner & Ziemann 2023, PMID:37743002) — is the open problem.

## Controversies the targets carry

- **Is the target's *depth* doing the work?** Pooled network meta-analyses do not show deep rTMS superior to cheaper, more focal high-frequency rTMS (e.g. 81 trials, "deep rTMS was not more effective than sham" while conventional HF-rTMS was; doi:10.1001/jamapsychiatry.2016.3644). If any advantage is real, the biophysics says it comes from **broader network engagement**, not deep-structure stimulation — which undercuts the name more than the method.
- **Precision vs breadth.** The very connectivity data that justify the DLPFC target (Fox 2012; Weigand 2018) also indict the *fixed* H1 field for not hitting each patient's optimal spot — non-personalised dTMS may leave response on the table.
- **Blinding.** The H-coil produces a strong scalp sensation; if sham does not reproduce it, unblinding inflates apparent effects — a standing threat to every effect size above.
- **A single-sponsor evidence base.** The H-coil is one manufacturer's (Brainsway); several completed pivotal trials have no results posted to ClinicalTrials.gov despite journal publication — a reason to weight independent and negative data more heavily.
