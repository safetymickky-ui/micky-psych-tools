# Pheochromocytoma & Paraganglioma MOC

Map of content for pheochromocytoma and sympathetic paraganglioma (PPGL) — catecholamine-secreting
chromaffin-cell tumours. First reports centre on the **perioperative window**, where the disease's
mortality concentrates: pharmacologic preparation (α-then-β blockade, volume loading), intra-operative
surge-then-crash management, and the post-operative hypotension/hypoglycaemia watch. New reports,
notes, and rendered references on PPGL hang here.

## Artifacts

- [[ppgl-perioperative-management]] — Phase-structured, sourced surgical-safety reference for elective
  resection of functional PPGL: pre-op α-blockade first (phenoxybenzamine 10 mg BID → 1 mg/kg/day or
  doxazosin 2 → 32 mg/day, PRESCRIPT RCT settling agent choice to "either"), the alpha-before-beta rule,
  targets (seated BP <130/80, HR 60–70) and sodium/fluid loading; intra-op surges (phentolamine,
  nicardipine, nitroglycerin, magnesium, esmolol) then post-ligation crash (fluids, norepinephrine,
  vasopressin); post-op 24–48 h watch for hypotension and rebound hypoglycaemia; risk stratification and
  a contraindicated-drugs safety set. PubMed 15 · trials 4; confidence moderate-high.
