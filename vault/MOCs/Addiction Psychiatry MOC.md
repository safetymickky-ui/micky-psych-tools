# Addiction Psychiatry MOC

Map of content for the addiction-service evidence base — decision reports, reviews, and
protocol work supporting the substance-use-disorder service. Seeded by the accreditation
research program (alcohol withdrawal, methamphetamine-associated psychosis, relapse-prevention
pharmacotherapy, smoking cessation, contingency management, retention, and service design).

## Artifacts

- [[Alcohol Withdrawal on a District-Hospital Ward — Protocol Choice and Thiamine Dosing]] — ward-standard regimen (risk-stratified benzodiazepine front-loading over open symptom-triggered dosing; phenobarbital as reserve) + parenteral thiamine dosing/sequencing. Confidence moderate-high on the skeleton.
- [[Methamphetamine-Associated Psychosis — Consult Protocol (Antipsychotic Choice, Duration, Transition Risk, LAI)]] — olanzapine/quetiapine first-line (avoid aripiprazole acutely), time-limited not indefinite; transition to schizophrenia ≈22%, watch 2–5 y; LAI reserved on borrowed (schizophrenia) evidence — no MAP-specific LAI trial exists. Feeds the SMI-V/violence layer.
- [[Smoking-Cessation Pharmacotherapy for a Counseling-Only Public Clinic — Varenicline vs NRT vs Bupropion vs Cytisine]] — add pharmacotherapy to counselling (≈doubles abstinence, ~11%→~20%+); cytisine first-line for a public system (≈varenicline efficacy, cheaper, fewer AEs; RAUORA), varenicline/combination NRT co-first-line, bupropion reserved; EAGLES cleared the neuropsychiatric fear — comorbidity is not a barrier.
- [[AUD Relapse-Prevention Pharmacotherapy with Limited Adherence Support — Naltrexone, Acamprosate, Disulfiram, and the Second Line]] — naltrexone first (once-daily/XR fits low support; NNT ~11–18), acamprosate co-first-line (abstinent/hepatic), disulfiram ONLY supervised (open-label artefact), second-line topiramate/gabapentin/baclofen (baclofen niche = liver disease).
- [[Methamphetamine Use Disorder Pharmacotherapy — What Is Usable Off-Label Now (ADAPT-2, Mirtazapine, Agonists) vs What to Wait For]] — nothing approved; ADAPT-2 combo the best off-label try-now (NEJM, effect 11.1 pp), mirtazapine reasonable adjunct, bupropion marginal, agonists watch; CM is the backbone; frontier = GLP-1 agonists/ketamine (2026–2030).
- [[Contingency Management for Stimulant Use Disorder — Effect Size, Design Parameters, and Low-Cost Adaptation for a Public System]] — build it: CM is the ONLY treatment reliably tied to stimulant abstinence (Bentzley OR 2.13; d≈0.7; De Crescenzo CM+CRA OR 2.84); design = objective target, adequate magnitude, immediacy, escalation+reset, 2–3×/wk, ≥12 wk; prize-based (fishbowl) for low cost; plan for decay. Prime R2R candidate.
- [[Retention Interventions in Outpatient SUD — What Works, How Retention Is Defined, and How to Defend a Pre/Post Retention Jump]] — best levers: attendance-CM, case management/outreach, structural supports (phones/flexible scheduling); MI & generic counselling NOT reliable for retention; no single retention definition (recommend retained-at-3/6-mo, held constant); the 30.9→79.8% jump is real but pre/post = association not causation (name RTM, definition drift, case-mix; strengthen with ITS/comparison). Second R2R candidate.
- [[Suicide Risk in Stimulant Users — What an 8Q-Positive Pathway Should Contain in a Non-Psychiatric Hospital]] — the screen isn't the intervention: stratify → immediate safety (means restriction/observation, don't discharge mid-crash) → brief structured intervention (safety plan alone is null — Albaum) → **sustained follow-up contact** (the load-bearing part — VigilanS/brief-contact) → addiction linkage. MA suicide-attempt risk aHR 1.80. Pathway you'll author.
- [[Violence Risk in SUD (SMI-V Layer) — How HCR-20 and V-RISK-10 Map onto SMI-V, and What the OAS Actually Measures vs Predicts]] — match tool to job: V-RISK-10 for the acute SMI-V screen, HCR-20 for complex/forensic; tools are **rule-out not rule-in** (Fazel NPV 91% / PPV 41% — not for sole detention); **OAS measures aggression after an incident, does not predict** (Eikelboom); substance misuse/non-adherence/impulsivity are the modifiable drivers (Witt). มาตรการที่ 3.
