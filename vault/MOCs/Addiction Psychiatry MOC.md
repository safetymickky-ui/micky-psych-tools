# Addiction Psychiatry MOC

Map of content for the addiction-service evidence base — decision reports, reviews, and
protocol work supporting the substance-use-disorder service. Home of the **accreditation
research program**, now complete: **16 decision-locked questions → 16 filed reports** across
Tier 1 (protocols with the owner's name on them), Tier 2 (service-design/R2R candidates), and
Tier 3 (context and strategy). Beyond the program, the MOC also carries **comprehensive
reviews** — whole-topic academic companions to the decision reports (first: the Matrix
intensive-outpatient review paired with the Matrix Model evidence-base report).

## Artifacts

- [[Alcohol Withdrawal on a District-Hospital Ward — Protocol Choice and Thiamine Dosing]] — ward-standard regimen (risk-stratified benzodiazepine front-loading over open symptom-triggered dosing; phenobarbital as reserve) + parenteral thiamine dosing/sequencing. Confidence moderate-high on the skeleton.
- [[Methamphetamine-Associated Psychosis — Consult Protocol (Antipsychotic Choice, Duration, Transition Risk, LAI)]] — olanzapine/quetiapine first-line (avoid aripiprazole acutely), time-limited not indefinite; transition to schizophrenia ≈22%, watch 2–5 y; LAI reserved on borrowed (schizophrenia) evidence — no MAP-specific LAI trial exists. Feeds the SMI-V/violence layer.
- [[Smoking-Cessation Pharmacotherapy for a Counseling-Only Public Clinic — Varenicline vs NRT vs Bupropion vs Cytisine]] — add pharmacotherapy to counselling (≈doubles abstinence, ~11%→~20%+); cytisine first-line for a public system (≈varenicline efficacy, cheaper, fewer AEs; RAUORA), varenicline/combination NRT co-first-line, bupropion reserved; EAGLES cleared the neuropsychiatric fear — comorbidity is not a barrier.
- [[AUD Relapse-Prevention Pharmacotherapy with Limited Adherence Support — Naltrexone, Acamprosate, Disulfiram, and the Second Line]] — naltrexone first (once-daily/XR fits low support; NNT ~11–18), acamprosate co-first-line (abstinent/hepatic), disulfiram ONLY supervised (open-label artefact), second-line topiramate/gabapentin/baclofen (baclofen niche = liver disease).
- [[Methamphetamine Use Disorder Pharmacotherapy — What Is Usable Off-Label Now (ADAPT-2, Mirtazapine, Agonists) vs What to Wait For]] — nothing approved; ADAPT-2 combo the best off-label try-now (NEJM, effect 11.1 pp), mirtazapine reasonable adjunct, bupropion marginal, agonists watch; CM is the backbone; frontier = GLP-1 agonists/ketamine (2026–2030).
- [[Contingency Management for Stimulant Use Disorder — Effect Size, Design Parameters, and Low-Cost Adaptation for a Public System]] — build it: CM is the ONLY treatment reliably tied to stimulant abstinence (Bentzley OR 2.13; d≈0.7; De Crescenzo CM+CRA OR 2.84); design = objective target, adequate magnitude, immediacy, escalation+reset, 2–3×/wk, ≥12 wk; prize-based (fishbowl) for low cost; plan for decay. Prime R2R candidate.
- [[Retention Interventions in Outpatient SUD — What Works, How Retention Is Defined, and How to Defend a Pre/Post Retention Jump]] — best levers: attendance-CM, case management/outreach, structural supports (phones/flexible scheduling); MI & generic counselling NOT reliable for retention; no single retention definition (recommend retained-at-3/6-mo, held constant); the 30.9→79.8% jump is real but pre/post = association not causation (name RTM, definition drift, case-mix; strengthen with ITS/comparison). Second R2R candidate.
- [[Suicide Risk in Stimulant Users — What an 8Q-Positive Pathway Should Contain in a Non-Psychiatric Hospital]] — the screen isn't the intervention: stratify → immediate safety (means restriction/observation, don't discharge mid-crash) → brief structured intervention (safety plan alone is null — Albaum) → **sustained follow-up contact** (the load-bearing part — VigilanS/brief-contact) → addiction linkage. MA suicide-attempt risk aHR 1.80. Pathway you'll author.
- [[Violence Risk in SUD (SMI-V Layer) — How HCR-20 and V-RISK-10 Map onto SMI-V, and What the OAS Actually Measures vs Predicts]] — match tool to job: V-RISK-10 for the acute SMI-V screen, HCR-20 for complex/forensic; tools are **rule-out not rule-in** (Fazel NPV 91% / PPV 41% — not for sole detention); **OAS measures aggression after an incident, does not predict** (Eikelboom); substance misuse/non-adherence/impulsivity are the modifiable drivers (Witt). มาตรการที่ 3.
- [[The Matrix Model Evidence Base — RCT Support, Comparative Efficacy, Local Adaptations, and the Honest Ceiling for Its Grade]] — honest ceiling: MTP RCT (n=978) shows Matrix > TAU **in-treatment only** (superiority did NOT persist at discharge/6 mo); not shown superior to CBT/MI/CRA (only CM+CRA reliably beats TAU); grade "Matrix-informed" on your own outcomes, not a borrowed III-4; add CM + continuing care to raise it.
- [[Matrix Intensive Outpatient Treatment — Comprehensive Review]] — the whole-topic academic companion to the evidence-base report: maps Matrix within the IOP/psychosocial landscape across a ten-section arc, weighted on **components & fidelity**. The model is a manualised *container* (CBT + MI + CM + 12-step + family) whose active ingredient is retention + in-treatment abstinence; IOP as a level of care ≈ inpatient (McCarty, evidence high); CM+CRA the only NMA winner (De Crescenzo OR 2.84); 2024 Cochrane — psychosocial cuts dropout (RR 0.82) but ≈ TAU on abstinence; fidelity is unmeasured in most real "Matrix" programs; future = augment/re-deliver the core (digital/peer CM, tDCS adjunct, Vietnam Matrix+CM). 26 PubMed + 8 trials.
- [[Short-Stay Residential vs Outpatient SUD Treatment — Is the Bed-Stay the Treatment, or Is the Aftercare? Reframing the Home Ward + 10-Visit Follow-Up]] — no: inpatient not superior to intensive outpatient for most (McKay/ASAM); the durable active ingredient is **continuing care** — but only when **longer + actively delivered**, higher-risk benefit most (McKay reviews). Reframe: 14 days = stabilisation/engagement; the **10-visit follow-up is the treatment** — protect its dose, make it active.
- [[Harm Reduction for Stimulant (Non-Opioid) Populations — What the Evidence Supports Beyond Needle Exchange]] — beyond NSP: **safer-smoking supplies** (crack/meth pipe kits — Frankeberger/Fitzpatrick), **drug checking** (fentanyl-adulterated supply + engagement — Santelices), **integrated sexual-health/HIV/PrEP**, naloxone; no proven stimulant agonist-substitution; evidence mostly observational — implement *with* measurement.
- [[Community-Based Drug Treatment (CBTx) Outcomes and the Quality-of-Life Instrument Choice (WHOQOL-BREF)]] — CBTx is a delivery *framework*; claim its components' evidence (CM/psychosocial/retention) + measure your own; task-shifted community treatment works in Thai low-resource (CETA/Bolton). **QoL instrument = WHOQOL-BREF** (Thai-validated, SUD-validated, free) but pair with a substance-specific outcome (generic QoL moves modestly).
- [[Quasi-Coerced Treatment Outcomes — Legally-Pressured (ม.114-Type Diversion) vs Self-Referral, and What Moderates It]] — depends on coercion type: **detention compulsory treatment = no benefit + harm** (Werb) — don't build that; **legal leverage** (autonomy preserved) retains/completes ≥ self-referral (Copeland); diverted stream = earlier/less-severe/lower internal motivation → same evidence-based treatment + front-load engagement (MI modest, #7).
- [[Emerging Local Substances — Ketamine (incl. Uropathy), Kratom + Cannabis Post-Legalization in Thailand, and Adolescent E-Cigarettes]] — three watch-items: **ketamine** → screen chronic users for uropathy, treat with abstinence + joint urology–addiction; **kratom/cannabis** → risk is adulterated kratom "cocktails" + cannabis perception–evidence gap/psychosis (Assanangkornchai Thai data), not traditional use; **adolescent vaping** → text-message cessation works (Graham JAMA RR 1.35) + school prevention, NOT adult pharmacotherapy.
- [[Outcome Indicators for an Addiction Service — A KPI Panel Grounded in the Evidence (Retention, Remission, Functioning, Safety)]] — no single adopted standard set; build a compact 4-domain panel (**retention** at ≥3/6 mo fixed-definition · **use/remission** · **functioning/QoL** = WHOQOL-BREF · **safety/harm**), mix process + outcome measures, hold each definition constant, keep it small. Answers the surveyor recommendation at the root.
