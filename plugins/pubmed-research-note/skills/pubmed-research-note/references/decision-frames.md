# Decision Frames

Four frames. Pick one in Step 0. The frame decides the verdict slot, which evidence is
load-bearing, what must be quantified, and which engines are mandatory.

The frame is not a template to fill. It is the answer to *what is this person about to do
differently.*

---

## Rx — treatment choice for a patient in front of you

**The question.** Do I give this, to this patient, at what dose, in what sequence?

**The verdict slot.** Give / don't give / give only if X fails first. A dose or range. An
explicit statement of what NOT to promise the patient.

**Load-bearing evidence.** Head-to-head trials over placebo trials. Effect size on the
endpoint the patient cares about, not the surrogate. NNT beside NNH. Time-to-effect.
Discontinuation rate in the treatment arm — the real-world proxy for tolerability.

**Must be quantified.** Effect size with CI. NNT and NNH. Dose range and titration
interval. Absolute event rates for the two or three harms that would stop you.

**Engines.** PubMed + **ClinicalTrials.gov mandatory** (a completed-unpublished negative
trial is the single most common reason a confident Rx verdict is wrong).

**The trap.** Efficacy in a specialist RCT population generalizing to a Klaeng OPD patient
with three comorbidities and no monitoring. Name the gap explicitly under
`## Where it breaks`.

## Service — build, change, or fund a service

**The question.** Is this worth standing up here, with the staff and money we have?

**The verdict slot.** Build / don't / build only under conditions C. Throughput and
resource implication stated in units the hospital uses.

**Load-bearing evidence.** Effectiveness (pragmatic, real-world) over efficacy
(explanatory RCT). Remission and durability, not response at week 6. Sessions required,
staff time per patient, equipment lifespan. Cost-effectiveness where it exists. Whether
the effect survives outside academic centres.

**Must be quantified.** Response and remission rates with n. Sessions per course.
Durability (relapse at 6 and 12 months). Staff-hours per completed course. Where possible,
cost per responder.

**Engines.** PubMed + **ClinicalTrials.gov mandatory** (an ongoing large trial reading out
inside your build horizon changes the build decision entirely, and a registry full of
industry-sponsored terminated trials is a signal in itself — `search_by_sponsor`).

**The trap.** Efficacy literature answering a question the service does not ask. A therapy
with `d = 0.6` and a 30-session course may be a worse service decision than one with
`d = 0.4` and six sessions. If the report does not compute what the service actually costs
per responder, it has answered the wrong question.

---

## Truth — is this claim real?

**The question.** Someone asserted X. Is X true, false, or unsettled?

**The verdict slot.** True / false / unsettled — plus **why the disagreement exists**,
which is the part the user actually needs. "Unsettled" is only acceptable when accompanied
by the specific experiment that would settle it.

**Load-bearing evidence.** The strongest study *for* and the strongest study *against*,
adjudicated head-to-head. Replication status. Effect-size drift across time (an effect
that shrinks with each larger trial is a dying effect, and saying so is the finding).
Preregistration. Funding source when it plausibly bears on the result.

**Must be quantified.** Effect sizes across the replication series, in order, with n.
Publication year. Where the confidence interval crosses null.

**Engines.** PubMed. ClinicalTrials.gov optional but high-yield — an unpublished completed
trial is direct evidence about *why* the published record looks the way it does.

**The trap.** Counting papers. Twelve small positive trials and one large rigorous negative
trial is not 12–1. Adjudicate by design and power, and say so in the prose.

---

## Teaching — what can you assert out loud to trainees?

**The question.** What sentence can I say in a lecture without being wrong in five years?

**The verdict slot.** The sentence itself, written out verbatim, as it should be spoken.
Then the sentence's expiry conditions.

**Load-bearing evidence.** The gap between what the canonical textbook says and what the
current evidence says. Trainees learn from the textbook and are examined on it; where the
two diverge, the report must name both positions and say which one the trainee should hold
and which one they should be able to *recognize* on an exam.

**Must be quantified.** Whatever number belongs in the spoken sentence — one number,
correct, memorable.

**Engines.** PubMed + **Open Library mandatory** (name the edition; a textbook claim
without an edition is not a textbook claim).

**The trap.** Teaching the evidence and leaving the trainee to fail the board question
written from the textbook. Flag the divergence, do not resolve it silently in favour of
the literature.

---

## When two frames both apply

Pick the frame whose **wrong answer costs more**, and declare it in one line under the
title. Rx beats Teaching. Service beats Truth. A report that straddles reverts to the
encyclopedia — which is the failure this skill exists to prevent.
